Early Life

Ray Charles was born in Georgia in 1930. He pioneered the genre soul music in the 1950s. His musical curiosity was sparked by man named Pitman who played boogie woogie on an old upright piano at his cafe. Afterward Pitman taught Ray how to play the piano himself and always welcomed Ray’s family to the cafe, as well as allowing them live there during financial troubles. He suffered a traumatic experience when his younger brother drowned right in front of his eyes. Soon after his brother death, he started to lose his vision and became completely blind by the age of 7. He went to the Florida School for the Deaf and the Blind in St.Augustine, Florida. This where he learned to read, write and arrange music in braille, this where he also learned to play piano, organ, sax, clarinet and trumpet.

Career

Ray Charles was fascinated in the jazz and blues music he hearn on the family radio. Charles's mother passed away when he was 15, so he toured the “Chitlin Circuit” in the South. Unfortunately when he was on tour he got addicted to heroin. When he turned 16, Ray moved to Seattle where Quincy Jones. Quincy was a friend and collaborator that he would keep for the rest of his life. Charles performed with the McSon Trio in 1940s that released a hit song called “ Confession of the Blues “ who also later changed their name to Maxin Trio. With a few years group he developed his musical talent and his musical style. In 1953, Charles got a deal with Atlantic Records and celebrated the deal with his first hit single “Mess Around”. The next year, Charles reached critical acclaim with his now classic song “I Got a Woman”. Back then the song reached number 1 on the R&B charts and demonstrated his new music style. He pioneered a genre of music call soul which is a fusion of gospel and R&B. His success continued, fellow musicians began to call Charles “The Genius” and his ability to cross over to pop with number 6 on the pop chart hit song “What’d I Say”. In the year 1960, Ray Charles got his first Grammy Award for “Georgia on My Mind” and following that he got another grammy for the single “Hit the Road, Jack”. But then he got addicted to heroin and got arrested for possession. But he was able to avoid jail by getting rid of his addiction at a L.A clinic.

Impact and Legacy

Ray Charles died on June 10, 2004 because he was suffering from liver disease. During his lifetime he performed more than 10,000 concerts and recorded more than 60 albums. Ray Charles possessed one of the most recognizable voice in American music and his unique music inspired many other artists like Elvis Presley and Stevie Wonder.
